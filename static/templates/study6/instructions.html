What is Your Task in this Game?

Your task in the game is to work with another partner (either another Mechanical Turk worker or an automated target detection system) to analyze 20 target vehicles on a map as quickly as possible in each of the five rounds of gameplay to identify dangerous vehicles.

This task represents a real-life scenario where an operator (You, in this study) needs to use drones to monitor a large area, and work together with another partner (either another human operator or an automated system) to identify vehicles as dangerous or non-dangerous. This can help to more efficiently stop the dangerous criminals.

Operator's Panel

You will control the drone at the operator's panel (the left panel). The drone always begins at the lower left corner of the panel. To inspect an image, you first select one of the blue markers on the map. After the drone arrives at the marker, the image will be shown in the vehicle identification panel on the right side of your screen. Use the "Zoom In" and "Zoom Out" buttons to inspect the image. Then, select "Yes" if the vehicle is dangerous or "No" if it is not. Then click on another marker to inspect the next image and repeat this process.

Partner's Panel

Your partner inspects the assigned images simultaneously. The drone at the partner's panel (the right panel) is controlled by your partner, and it moves between orange markers on the map. Partner's drone always begins at the lower right corner of the panel.

Allocating Images to the Partner

For each round, you will decide how many images to allocate to your partner to try to improve your score.

Score Calculation

If targets are not quickly identified, criminals may be able to harm people before their vehicles are identified.

If targets are incorrectly identified, resources may be inefficiently devoted to stopping innocent people instead of criminals.

As a result, both speed and accuracy are important, and your Score for each round is based on both the speed and accuracy with which images are identified collectively by you and your partner.

The score is calculated as follows:
Round score = (Accuracy Score + Time Score) / 2
Accuracy Score is calculated based on you and your partner's total correct identifications.
Time Score is calculated based on the time it takes to finish a round.
